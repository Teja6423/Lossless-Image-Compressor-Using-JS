Image Compression

When it comes to digital files, compression refers to encoding information using fewer bits than the original file. Essentially, this means taking a large file and turning it into a smaller one. There are two types of compression: lossless and lossy. Lossless compression involves removing any empty, needless, or duplicated bits from the original file. This results in a smaller file that maintains the same quality as the original. On the other hand, lossy compression involves removing excessive or unimportant bits from the original file. This creates a smaller file but with a reduction in quality. With lossy compression, the degree to which you want to reduce the file size depends on how much quality loss you're willing to tolerate.


Why would you Compress your Images?

Images from certain sources, such as professional DSLR cameras, can have very large file sizes, sometimes reaching dozens of megabytes in the case of a JPG file. Depending on your needs, this might be too large. In such cases, compressing the image can be very useful. You may also have large images on your phone, which could be consuming a significant amount of storage space and limiting your ability to take more photos. Compressing these images can free up internal storage and help solve this problem.
